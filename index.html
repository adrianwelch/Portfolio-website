About me

After graduating with a Business Management degree from The University of Birmingham in 2015, I decided to take a year off before starting my career, to travel and gain inspiration of where my path would lie next.

However I was still unsure which direction to take so took an internship opportunity in corporate events. After a few months, I knew this wasn't for me and an important thing I learned during my travels was to do something that makes me truly happy.

I quickly became fascinated by coding through my research of the tech industry. The constant problem solving and unlimited creativity involved in coding was what really inticed me to start learning how to code myself. I took online courses and learnt basic code. This has led me to enroll in a Web Development Immersive program with the intention to gain more technical skills and further my career in the tech industry. There, I devoted twelve hours a day to Javascript and Ruby coding challenges, elaborate assignments using Rails and Node, and projects that allowed me to practice what I enjoy most.
